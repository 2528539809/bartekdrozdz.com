In 2010, I was working with North Kingdom on an interactive installation called Woodbot Pilots.

Woodbot Pilots was a gesture controlled racing game running on a huge 82″ HD touch screen (191 x 123 cm!). We used a special 3D depth camera to read the gestures. This was before anybody knew what Kinect was! The camera was developed by a company called Fotonic. It had a range up to 7m/21ft, optimized for industrial environments.

The installation was deployed at the Skellefteå Airport in Northern Sweden in the main departures area.

The talented artists from Ars Thanea and North Kingdom created beautiful visuals and Dinamhoe added a great soundtrack. The game itself was developed with Unity3D. We connected the 3D camera using a C/C++ plugin developed by Interactive Institute in Umeå who did a fantastic job – it did run very smoothly.
